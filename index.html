Tips For Managing Stress as a Journalist

Assembly time: Change over time
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

As a journalist, do you find your self riddled with anxiety over deadlines, interviews, big projects, and day-to-day work tasks? Well here are a few small changes that you can make to your life so relieve some of this unnecessary stress!

  • Things You'll Need

  • A To Do list
  • Tennis shoes
  • A positive attitude

  1. Make a list of tasks to do.

    Having a task list in front of you will help you see which have higher priority than others. Do these tasks first and systematically cross other items off your list.

  2. Pause before you take on more work.

    If you plan to volunteer to take on more work, or you are asked to add an extra project, pause for a minute to consider what this will do to your current workload. Calculate how much time you are devoting to various projects and figure out if you have extra time to handle more.

    " >

    A journalist overwhelmed by his workload.

  3. Have realistic expectations.

    Understanding what can be realistically accomplished in a given time frame will help you reframe your expectations. If you find that your expectations are not being met, think about how you might adjust deadlines and project goals.

  4. Find journalist friends.

    Having people in your corner can help you handle stress. It can help to have someone to talk to, and who can push for your best interests.

  5. Get 7-8 hours of sleep every night.

    When you are not well-rested, you are not as well-prepared to handle stress. Aim to get around 7-8 hours of sleep every night to ensure that you feel tip-top in the morning.

  6. Exercise regularly.

    Fend off tension and stress by exercising a few times a week. Go for a jog or hit the gym to work off extra negative energy caused by stress.

  7. Try meditation.

    Taking time every day to meditate, even for 5-10 minutes, can help reduce stress levels and reduce negative thinking.
